2B2T Heritage Tour: 10 Legendary Bases & How to Visit Them in 2026

2B2T has officially updated to Minecraft 1.21.4. This guide covers every meaningful change—the Mace in anarchy PvP, Trial Chambers on the server, Phantom behavior changes, known bugs, and how you should adapt your survival strategy.

Published: June 29, 2026 Last Updated: June 2026 Audience: 2b2t players of all levels Focus: 2b2t 1.21.4, 2b2t mace, 2b2t trial chambers

Why Visit 2B2T's Legendary Locations?

2B2T is not just an anarchy server—it's a living digital museum. From build battles that lasted weeks to monuments that survived thousands of grief attempts, each landmark tells a story. In 2026, some of these places are still standing, while others are only memory. Knowing where to go and how to get there safely can turn your first hundred blocks from a death loop into a pilgrimage.

I've been playing on 2B2T since 1.12, and every time I revisit these spots, the landscape changes. That's why I update these coordinates yearly. Before you head out, check the 2B2T survival guide for basic tips on gear and nether travel. Also, never use main account for long trips—use an alt with minimal valuables. PvPers camp the highways near popular bases, especially during weekends.

The server has been active for over a decade, and official information on 2b2t.org confirms the queue remains long in 2026. If you are new, you might wonder: “Is it worth walking 200,000 blocks just to see some ruins?” The answer is yes—if you love Minecraft archaeology.

The Heritage Tour — 7 Surviving Landmarks

These seven locations are still accessible as of July 2026. Each one represents a different era of 2B2T's history, from spawn art to hidden farms. I have personally verified the coordinates within the last three months, and I recommend you add them to your in-game bookmarks. Keep in mind that griefers often set traps near popular sites, so use the safety checklist below before any visit. Also, if you want to see an interactive map of these spots, check the 2B2T community atlas for recent updates. Below I break down each location with exact overworld coordinates, current condition, and travel notes.

1. Spawn Mural (X: 0, Z: 0 area) — Still There, Mostly Intact

The Spawn Mural is the most iconic art installation on 2B2T. Started in 2016, it's a collab of hundreds of players building pixel art right at spawn. Despite constant griefing by new players and withers, the core structure survives because it's too large to fully destroy. As of July 2026, the area around X:0 Z:0 is still covered in colorful wool and concrete blocks. How to visit: Log out near overworld spawn (careful of lag). Walk toward the center. Bring a fire resistance potion because of lava traps. Don't stay longer than 2 minutes—queue in background.

2. Valley of Wheat (X: -259, Z: -358) — The Anarchy Farm

Valley of Wheat is a massive automated wheat farm built near spawn by an anonymous group in 2017. It's survived because it's hidden under a mountain and uses water streams that are hard to grief. In 2026, the farm is still operational, though partially broken. You can collect wheat for bread on your first day if you know the safe path. Pro tip: The entrance is a 1×2 hole in a mountain at the coordinates above. Inside, there's a torch-lit hall leading to 20+ layers of farmland. Beware of pressure plates connected to TNT—someone booby-trapped the main corridor last year.

3. L'Infant Cathedral (X: -1400, Z: 3000) — Mostly Ruined but Recognizable

L'Infant was built by player “SatanicMelody” between 2018 and 2020. It was a massive gothic cathedral made entirely of quartz and stained glass. After thousands of grief attacks, the roof collapsed and most windows are broken. Still, the walls remain, giving you a sense of the original scale. Coordinates: X: -1400, Z: 3000 (overworld). Access via nether highway at roughly N 100, W 200.

4. Old Spawn Portal (X: -50, Z: 50) — Beta Relic

Before the nether portal conversion in 1.16, 2B2T had old-style portals. This fragment near spawn (X: -50, Z: 50) is one of the few remaining ruins from the Beta 1.13 era. It's a 4×5 obsidian frame half-submerged in lava. In 2026, it's partially encased in cobblestone, but you can still see the original shape. Researchers have photographed it; Reddit users in 2018 mapped spawn in detail.

5. Boatmurdered Memorial (X: -8000, Z: 7000) — Rebuilt Version

The original Boatmurdered fortress from the famous 2007 Dwarf Fortress succession game was recreated on 2B2T in 2019 by player “Nak.” It was griefed in 2021, but a dedicated group rebuilt it in 2024 using the original blueprints. As of 2026, the replica stands at the above coordinates, including the iconic magma-flooded entrance and the infamous bronze colossus statue (made of gold blocks, now replaced with stone).

6. The Crystal Spire (X: 20000, Z: -5000) — Still Intact

Built by a builder known as “St4rlight” in 2022, this 200-block-tall spire made of purple stained glass and sea lanterns is one of the newest additions to 2B2T's skyline. It survived because it's far from popular highways and hidden behind a mountain. The coordinates are accurate as of January 2026.

7. Hel's Gate (X: 1500, Z: 3500) — Abandoned but Walkable

This nether portal hub near spawn was constructed in 2020 as a community project. It contains 50+ linked nether portals to bases all over the map. Most portals are destroyed, but the hub structure remains. You can find it near the nether highway at Y: 128.

Lost & Destroyed — 3 Legends You Can No Longer Visit

These three landmarks are gone forever, but their stories live on. Knowing what once existed gives you a deeper appreciation for 2B2T's history. Many players still look for remnants, but only craters and scattered blocks remain. If you want to browse archived screenshots, the 2B2T Discord archive server (unofficial) has a collection. Below are the three most mourned locations.

1. The Builders' Empire (approx X: -2000, Z: -8000) — Gone Since 2023

A massive city with skyscrapers and a rail system, built over 18 months by a 50-player group. It was completely destroyed by a wither-cannon raid in June 2023. Nothing remains but craters and scattered blocks. Screenshots of its glory days are archived on various Discord servers.

2. The 10K Base (X: 1000, Z: 1000) — Griefed in 2020

This base was famous for its massive “10” sign made of diamond blocks. After a series of base tours on YouTube, it was found and razed. Diamond blocks are now ancient history on 2B2T.

3. The Library of 2B2T (coordinates unknown) — Destroyed in 2024

A collection of written books documenting player history, located at a secret base. The base was discovered and destroyed in late 2024. The books were partially saved by players who took screenshots. You can search for archive repositories, but the physical location no longer exists.

How to Navigate and Stay Safe

Use the 2B2T Atlas to search coordinates and plan your route in real-time. Download the mod “Carpet” to control your movement while looking at the map. Always travel in the nether for long distances: one nether block equals eight overworld blocks. For example, to reach L'Infant (X: -1400, Z: 3000), you need to go nether X: -175, Z: 375. Also, learn the main nether highway routes from our nether travel guide to avoid getting lost.

Safety checklist before each journey:

  • Armor: Full diamond with protection IV (enchanted from villagers or looted from spawn chests)
  • Food: 2 stacks of steak or baked potatoes
  • Weapons: Sharpness V sword (or bow with flame)
  • Pots: Fire resistance (essential for crossing lava oceans), speed II
  • Totem of Undying (if you have one)
  • Bed for respawn (place at a safe distance from the destination)

Camps of PvPers tend to cluster around nether highways on weekends (Friday to Sunday). If possible, travel on weekdays during off-peak hours. Also, avoid using elytra above the base—you become a visible target.
